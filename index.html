Story Style Brand

Why Corporate Results Are a Matter of Personal Style.

Marketing, at its core, is simply about storytelling. It’s the ability to take consumers on an emotional journey that can relate to their needs and wants while, at the same time, establishing trust in the products and services that are being provided.

Trust is an emotional response and storytelling is the most essential element in establishing it.

How clients appear - including what they wear - is the first step and, arguably, the most critical step of the story they tell an audience. Media coaching and narrative development completely prepare a client to deliver a flawless, on-message interview, but the story needs to be aligned with the brand. Many executives forget to invest in what creates their brand. In a new era when brand success depends on its ability to establish trust and loyalty from its consumers - this kind of investment has become a missing link in brand development because it requires vulnerability, and most executive clients are conditioned to exude confidence at all times.

The JD Methodology was developed on the belief that by understanding and embracing human behavior, we, as a whole, have the opportunity to use our DNA to our advantage. "First impressions" are a direct, not to mention instant, response to our basic human nature. In a single moment, our emotional and visceral response overpowers any and all logic. Studies show that 95% of consumer decisions are made on emotion--and that includes their decision to trust your brand...and your leader.

The JD Methodology is about the ability to control the conversation with aligning brand and storytelling, which, in turn, establishes an emotional connection to all senses, whether the audience knows it or not.

How it works - The JD Method

The JD Method establishes the alignment of brand and storytelling, and gives leadership in companies like Salesforce, Facebook and LinkedIn the ability to capitalize on what matters most: Trust.

I have worked with dozens of executive clients to change their mindset of what style and brand means and created a process that begins with intention and authenticity. It is a disrupted approach that changes the way they see their wardrobe and their sense of style. This mindshift changes their first question of the day from ‘what to wear’ to ‘what is the story, the audience and the outcome for the day.’ It starts with a simple question: “What conversation do you want to have?”

The First Three Steps

The JD Method changes the way clients decide what to wear everyday. Starting from the inside out, focusing on what conversation they want to have and what story will resonate with an audience. The process is built upon three simple steps.

  1. The Story
  2. The Style
  3. The Brand

The Client Experience

  • Story & Brand development in partnership with Teneo.
  • Provide on-call strategy support with Teneo during first three months of brand launch.
  • Work directly with client (in their closet) to understand who they are as a person, a leader and the values and vision for the company.
  • Define their professional story, understand buying behavior, learn current process in building a look and make adjustments to their wardrobe, if needed.
  • Provided on-site support for media events, photoshoots, etc.
  • Build an exclusive set of (5) digital looks for media event from clients personal wardrobe as an additional level of support during initial project phase.
  • Build a foundation and authentic story for strategic alignment between leader and corporate brand.
